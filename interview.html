Damaso Bangaoet Jr.

"The Man Who Never Ran Out of Ideas"

It all began when lawyer, Dammy Bangaoet Jr., John Hay Poro Point Development Corporation (JPDC) Managing Director for Camp John Hay, presented to the Board of Directors of JPDC the idea of their spearheading the holding of a flower festival in Baguio City.

The Panagbenga is among his ideas that he loved the most. Its celebration has been successful over the past years in bringing in tourists. Bangaoet, otherwise known as "Dammy", is known as the "Father of Panagbenga" which he found in 1995, but to some, he is more than just that.

Along with other like-minded people, Bangaoet's love for Baguio City had a corresponding passion not only in making it the beautiful Baguio that we have, but in making it as a tourist destination. His thoughts have always been about how history, culture and nature could be used in tourism which will make other people appreciate them. For the people of Baguio, as long as the flowers bloom and this season is annually celebrated through the Panagbenga, the spirit of the man behind it will always be there.

Ruben Zaparita

One of the people who contributed to the success of Panagbenga 2015 is Mr. Zaparita, an active participant of the festival and a fellow Baguio citizen. As said in the interview, his father was part of several landscaping competitions, but back then, there were only two categories, the open category and the Cordillera themed category. As of now, some changes have been made, so the open category, vertical landscape and the carpet of flowers took place over the two earlier categories.

Mauricio Domogan

Mauricio G. Domogan is a man full of determination and perseverance. He is a servant leader type of politician and served his people 1988-1992 as a Councilor of the city of Baguio. Impressed by his humility, honesty, and genuine service, the people of Baguio elected him Vice-Mayor in May 1992. Being barred by the constitution to run for a fourth consecutive term in the Congress, he successfully reclaimed his post as Mayor of Baguio City for the 2010 election.

As the mayor of Baguio City and head of the organization for the Panagbenga festival, we asked him a few questions about his opinions and involvements in the city festival. He answered with great enthusiasm and has provided us information that we cannot find in books and news articles.
